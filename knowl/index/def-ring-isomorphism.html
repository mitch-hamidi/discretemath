Definition 16.1.13. Ring Isomorphism.

Let \([R; + , \cdot ]\) and \([R'; +', \cdot']\) be rings. Then \(R\) is isomorphic to \(R'\) if and only if there exists a function, \(f:R \to R'\text{,}\) called a ring isomorphism, such that
  1. \(f\) is a bijection
  2. \(f(a + b) =f(a)+'f(b)\) for all \(a, b \in R\)
  3. \(f(a \cdot b) = f(a)\cdot ' f(b)\) for all \(a,b \in R\text{.}\)
