Example 15.4.23.

Let \(G\) be the same group of two by two invertible real matrices as in Example 15.4.11. Define \(\Phi: G \rightarrow G\) by \(\Phi(A) = \frac{A}{\sqrt{\lvert \det A \rvert }}\text{.}\) We will let the reader verify that \(\Phi\) is a homomorphism. The theorems above imply the following.
  • \(\ker \Phi = \{A\in G |\Phi (A) =I\} = \left\{\left( \begin{array}{cc} a & 0 \\ 0 & a \\ \end{array} \right) \mid a\in \mathbb{R},a\neq 0\right\}\triangleleft G\text{.}\) This verifies our statement in Example 15.4.11. As in that example, let \(\ker \Phi = H_1\text{.}\)
  • \(G\left/H_1\right.\) is isomorphic to \(\{A \in G \mid \det A= 1\}\text{.}\)
  • \(\pi: G \rightarrow G\left/H_1\right.\) defined, naturally, by \(\pi(A) =A H_1\) is a homomorphism.
