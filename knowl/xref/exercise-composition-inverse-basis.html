Exercise 12.

Let \(f\) and \(g\) be functions whose inverses exist. Prove that \((f\circ g)^{-1}= g^{-1}\circ f^{-1}\text{.}\)
Hint.
See Exercise 3 of Section 5.4.
Answer.
Weâ€™ve proven that inverses are unique and so all we need to do is to demonstrate that \(g^{-1}\circ f^{-1}\) inverts \((f\circ g)\) to be able to equate it with \((f\circ g)^{-1}\text{.}\)
\begin{equation*} \begin{split} (f\circ g)\circ (g^{-1}\circ f^{-1})&=((f\circ g)\circ g^{-1})\circ f^{-1}\\ &=(f\circ (g\circ g^{-1}))\circ f^{-1}\\ &=(f\circ i)\circ f^{-1}\\ &=f\circ f^{-1}=i\\ \end{split} \end{equation*}
We can verify that \((g^{-1}\circ f^{-1}) \circ (f\circ g)\) is also equal to \(i\) with similar logic.
